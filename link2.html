Factors Influencing the Mathematics Percentage Achieved by

the Students



Students Approach on Learning Maths Influencing the Percentage of the Subject

From the Categorical distribution of bar plot,we can observe the following:-

  • Maths is Difficult: Not much variations is seen between agreeing/disagreeing maths is difficult and percentage rate of maths
  • Solve Maths: Students who agree to solve maths has 1.5 percent more in maths performance compared to those who disagrees.
  • Solve Maths in Groups: Students who agree to solve maths in groups has 1.5 percent more in maths performance compared to those who disagrees.
  • Draw Geometry: Students who agree to draw geometry has 1.5 percent more in maths performance compared to those who disagrees
  • Explain Answers: Students who agree to explain answers has 2 percent more in maths performance compared to those who disagrees


Effect of Maths Home Work on Students Maths Percentage

From the Categorical distribution of bar plot,we can observe the following:-

  • Giving Maths HW: Giving maths home work daily seems to have 3.5 percent more in maths performance compared to not giving once.
  • Correcting Maths HW: Correction of Maths home work daily has 3.5 percent more in maths performance compared to not correcting once.


Effect of Age, Gender, Siblings and Category of Student on Maths Percentage Scored

From the Categorical distribution of bar plot,we can observe the following:-

  • Age: Age group 1 to 14 has the maximum percentage rate and 16+ age group has the least percentage rate of maths.
  • Gender: Boys and girls seems to similar in percentage rate of maths.
  • Category: Overall Performance increases in the order of Category 3>4>2>1>0.
  • Siblings: Student having three or more Siblings tend to peform more in maths than the students with siblings less than three.


Effect of Reading a Book or Magazine, Helping in the Household and Playing Games on Maths percentage Scored

From the Categorical distribution of bar plot,we can observe the following:-

  • Read a Book: Students who read books once a week has 2 percent more in maths performance compared to those who read books eveyday.
  • Read Magazine: Students who read magazine everyday has 1.5 percent more in maths performance compared to those who never read.
  • Help in Household: Students who help in household everyday has 5 percent more in maths performance compared to those who never help in house holds.
  • Play Games: Students who play games has 3 percent more in maths performance compared to those who never play games.


Effect of Usage of Computers and Library, Subjects and Liking School on Maths Percentage Scored

From the Categorical distribution of bar plot,we can observe the following:-

  • Computer Use: Students who have no computer to use has 6.5 percent more in maths performance compared to those who never use computer.
  • Library Use: Students who use library more than once in a week has 3 percent more in maths performance compared to those who never use the library.
  • Subjects: Students who have Mathematics as subject has 5 percent more in maths performance compared to those who have no subjects.
  • Like School: Students who like school has 1.5 percent more in maths performance compared to those who do notlike the school.
