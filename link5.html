Effect of Social Studies Home Work on Students Social Studies Percentage



Observations and Insights

  • Giving Social Studies HW: Giving Social Studies home work 3-4 times a week seems to have 3.2 percent more in Social Studies performance compared to giving once.
  • Correcting Social Studies HW: Correction of Social Studies home work 1-2 times a week has 3.3 percent more in Social Studies performance compared to not correcting once.

Effect of Age, Gender, Siblings and Category of Student on Social Studies Percentage Scored



Observations and Insights

  • Age: Age group 13 has the maximum percentage rate and 16+ age group has the least percentage rate of Social Studies.
  • Gender: Boys and girls seems to similar in percentage rate of Social Studies
  • Category: Overall Performance increases in the order of Category 3>4>2>1>0.
  • Siblings: Student having one Sibling tend to peform more in Social Studies than the students with siblings more than three.

Effect of Usage of Computers and Library, Subjects and Liking School on Social Studies Percentage Scored



Observations and Insights

  • Computer Use: Students who have no computer has 4 percent more in Social Studies performance compared to those who never use computer.
  • Library Use: Students who use library more than once or twice in a month has 2 percent more in Social Studies performance compared to those who never use the library.
  • Subjects: Students who have Mathematics as subject has 4.5 percent more in Social Studies performance compared to those who have no subjects.
  • Like School: Students who like school has 2.8 percent more in Social Studies performance compared to those who do notlike the school.
